Welcome! I am a Ph.D. student in Political Science at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. My research focuses on American Politics, with an emphasis on Congress, campaigns, and machine learning. My dissertation argues that the electoral and institutional incentives that once aligned in Congress have diverged: for members oriented toward a national partisan audience rather than their district, the behaviors that maximize reelection are increasingly disconnected from the behaviors that produce legislation. 

I hold an MA in Political Science from UNC (2025) and a BA in Political Science from The Ohio State University (2023), where I received the Henry R. Spencer Award for Outstanding Honors Thesis in Political Science. I currently serve as Lead Data Architect for the State Health Policy Project (SHPP) at Boston University School of Public Health, where our team tracks state legislative bills to study the adoption of health policies.
